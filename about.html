Our Story

Matcha that
doesn't take itself
too seriously.

We started matcha-haha because the best cup we ever had came with a laugh, not a lecture.

In 2022, two friends spent six months walking through tea farms in Uji, Yame, Wazuka, Nishio and Kagoshima. They came back with a notebook full of tasting notes, a duffel bag of tins, and one shared conviction: matcha had become too precious.

So we built matcha-haha. A name that's a little silly, a logo that doesn't take itself too seriously, and a product that absolutely does. The matcha is ceremonial-grade. The mood is morning-laughter.

Sourcing

We work directly with five farms across Japan. We visit twice a year — once for the spring harvest, once to taste the new season's tencha before milling. No middlemen, no aggregators, no anonymous lots.

Each tin tells you the farm, the cultivar, the harvest date, and the date it was stone-milled. Because matcha is at its best in the first month after milling — and we'd rather you know that than not.

A Tea Leaf Initiative

Every kilo of matcha we mill leaves behind a small mountain of stems, veins, and fines that aren't fine enough for the tin. We were throwing them out. Then we stopped.

Today, those leftovers become incense (with a Tokyo studio), a celadon ceramic glaze (with our chawan maker), and an absurdly serious-looking bar soap. We don't make money on these — they cover their own costs. The goal is zero matcha in the bin.

Our Cafés

We started in a 14-seat space in Tiong Bahru. We now have six cafés in Singapore, two in Tokyo, and one in Taipei. Each one is a little different — same matcha, same whisk, different room.

Come for the matcha. Stay because the espresso machine is suspiciously good. Leave with a tin and, hopefully, a smile.
